AI-Assisted Autism Screener

Thank you for visiting.

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects an individual's sensory, language, and social processing abilities. Symptoms of ASD can vary widely from person to person and range from barely noticeable to extremely challenging, which is why people studying and working with those affected by ASD began to refer to ASD as a spectrum. Most children with ASD will show symptoms by 12-18 months--often earlier. Thanks to increased awareness in the past 20 years, infants and children are being diagnosed earlier now than ever before. This is good news, because early diagnosis and intervention can mitigate some of the difficulties faced by people with ASD and their families; early, high-quality intervention can make the difference between needing care for the rest of one's life and being an independent, productive adult. Even individuals with the most severe of symptoms can benefit from therapy to help them communicate their needs, perform self-care activities, and ensure their own safety and well-being.

The following questions have been adapted from the Q-CHAT-10, a well-known autism screener for infants, toddlers, and young children. If you have concerns that your infant or toddler may not be developing typically, this short survey may help you decide whether to seek further medical evaluation for your child. A point/threshold system is usually used to score and obtain results for this test; here, however, a machine learning model has been developed to analyze your responses and produce a recommendation based on data from more than 1,000 infants and toddlers who have been screened in the past. Please keep in mind that it's never wrong to discuss your concerns with your child's physician, even if your results here don't advise you to do so.
